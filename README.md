# Good, Bad and Ugly: Characterization of Healthy vs Diseased vs Tumor Microbiome

> Investigating gut microbiome differences across global populations and linking microbial signatures to health traits.

---

## Overview

The human gut microbiome serves as a biological fingerprint — unique to both the individual and the cultural group. Shaped by environment, diet, and lifestyle, these microbial communities may reflect the distinct health outcomes of different civilizations.

This project investigates variations in gut microbiome composition across populations worldwide and identifies microbial biomarkers that distinguish groups. By linking these signatures to specific health traits, the goal is to evaluate their potential overrepresentation in disease profiles.

---

## Research Questions

- How does the gut microbiome composition vary between different populations across the globe?
- Can specific microbial "signatures" or biomarkers be identified that are specific to a group?
- Can the identified bacterial signatures be linked to specific health traits?

---

## Objectives

| # | Objective | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Data Comparison** | Identify and compare microbial differences between different countries |
| 2 | **Signature Analysis** | Determine if there are microbial markers specific to different groups |
| 3 | **Bacteria Characterization** | Determine the functions of specific bacteria and their link to different traits |
| 4 | **Clinical Correlation** | Evaluate whether identified microbial markers are overrepresented in specific disease profiles |
